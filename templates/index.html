About the Project

This project was developed to offer a comprehensive analysis of how COVID-19 has affected individuals' BMI. Leveraging a robust dataset and state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms, our tool provides accurate predictions and valuable insights into weight trends during and after the pandemic.

Whether you are a healthcare professional, researcher, or simply curious about your health, the COVID BMI Predictor is designed to help you understand the impact of lifestyle changes brought on by the pandemic.

Explore our research papers, prediction tools, and interactive visualizations to learn more about the methodologies and findings behind this initiative.

BMI Limitations

BMI does not directly measure body fat and may not be fully accurate for athletes, the elderly, or certain ethnic groups. It is one of many health indicators.

COVID-19 Impact on BMI

Physical Activity Changes

Lockdowns and restrictions led to reduced physical activity and increased sedentary behavior, contributing to weight gain in many individuals.

Dietary Changes

Stress eating and limited access to fresh foods during lockdowns affected eating habits and weight management.

Direct Disease Impact

COVID-19 infection can affect metabolism, appetite, and muscle mass, leading to notable weight changes.

Mental Health Effects

Increased anxiety, depression, and stress during the pandemic influenced overall eating patterns and health behaviors.
